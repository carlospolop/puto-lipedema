Additional targeted findings while waiting:

- GLP-1/tirzepatide constipation: web/practice sources repeatedly mention slower GI motility, reduced intake/thirst, and prevention with scheduled fluids, gradual soluble fiber, movement after meals, and early PEG/macrogol if constipation appears. Source quality was mostly clinic/practice pages rather than high-grade trials; use as practical safety context, not primary evidence.
- Low-carb/keto constipation: sources are more weak/consumer-oriented, but consistent practical pattern: constipation often follows reduced plant fiber, lower food volume, fluid/electrolyte shifts, and sometimes excess cheese/low vegetables. Psyllium/PHGG are low-net-carb options; magnesium may help but renal/medication cautions remain.
- Dyssynergic defecation/pelvic floor: stronger clinical evidence. Symptoms: excessive straining, incomplete evacuation, anorectal blockage, digital maneuvers, bloating, hard stools; diagnosis uses anorectal manometry/balloon expulsion/defecography. Biofeedback is repeatedly described as superior/first-line for dyssynergia; do not keep escalating fiber when these clues dominate.
- ForumScout results were noisy but echoed patient patterns: GLP-1 and constipation communities often discuss psyllium, magnesium, prunes/kiwi, water, but also show red flags like dehydration, multiple constipating meds, prolapse/pelvic floor issues, and fiber intolerance.

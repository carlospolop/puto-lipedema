# Primary literature notes: LCHF/low-carb 2026 in lipedema

## PMID 42123982 / PMCID PMC13164597
**Title:** Changes in Vascular, Lymphatic, Inflammatory, and Lipid Mediators During a 7-Month Calorie-Restricted Low-Carbohydrate, High-Fat Dietary Intervention in Women with Lipedema: A Preliminary Prospective Study. DOI: 10.3390/nu18091381.

- Design: preliminary prospective study, no control group.
- Sample: 24 women with lipedema, median age 39.
- Intervention: 7-month individualized calorie-restricted LCHF diet under medical supervision.
- Outcomes: BMI, leg volume, adipose tissue pain (VAS), fasting serum mediators via Luminex and LC-MS/MS.
- Clinical results: significant reductions in BMI, leg volume and adipose tissue pain (p<0.001).
- Mediators: increased VEGF-A, VEGF-D and Ang-2; decreased pro-inflammatory cytokines and endothelial adhesion molecules. Endocannabinoid-related lipid mediators OEA, AEA, PEA decreased.
- Associations: baseline OEA/AEA and OEA reductions associated with greater BMI reduction; IL-8 change nominally associated with leg volume; pain improvement associated with decreases in P-selectin and VEGF-A and increases in IL-13.
- Interpretation: promising systemic changes during supervised calorie-restricted Mediterranean-style LCHF, but cannot infer causality or isolate carbs vs calorie deficit vs weight loss vs diet quality.

## PMID 41808123 / PMCID PMC13088385
**Title:** Changes in plasma fatty acid composition in females with lipedema following low-carbohydrate vs low-fat diets and associations with pain reduction. DOI: 10.1186/s12937-026-01304-y.

- Design: secondary analysis of randomized NCT04632810/LIPODIET; 8 weeks.
- Sample: 70 females with lipedema and obesity, BMI 30–45 kg/m2, mean age 47.
- Interventions: isocaloric low-energy low-carbohydrate diet vs low-fat diet.
- Findings: both groups decreased several saturated and polyunsaturated fatty acids; LCD-only reductions in arachidic, palmitoleic and oleic acids; low-fat-only increase lignoceric and decrease linoleic acid.
- Pain: changes in myristic and palmitic saturated fatty acids positively associated with changes in pain; reductions in SFAs, especially myristic acid, may relate to pain reduction in the LCD group.
- Interpretation: supports avoiding a high-saturated-fat “dirty keto” interpretation; best practical reading is low-carb Mediterranean with unsaturated fats and protein/fiber, not bacon/butter/cheese as staples.

## PMID 39408242 / PMCID PMC11478561
**Title:** The Efficacy of Ketogenic Diets (Low Carbohydrate; High Fat) as a Potential Nutritional Intervention for Lipedema: A Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis. DOI: 10.3390/nu16193276.

- Seven studies, 329 female participants, mean intervention duration ~15.85 weeks; all fair quality on Newcastle-Ottawa Scale.
- Found significant reductions in BMI, body weight, waist/hip circumferences and pain sensitivity.
- Limitations: few studies, small participants, heterogeneity, many pre-post/non-blinded designs. Helpful signal, not proof of cure.

## PMID 35949278 / PMCID PMC9358738
**Title:** Effect of a ketogenic diet on pain and quality of life in patients with lipedema: The LIPODIET pilot study. DOI: 10.1002/osp4.580.

- Pilot: 9 women, 7 weeks eucaloric LCHF followed by 6 weeks Nordic nutrition recommendations.
- LCHF: weight loss -4.6 kg and pain reduction -2.3 cm VAS; no correlation between weight loss and pain change at week 7.
- Pain returned to baseline after switching away from LCHF, while weight loss maintained.
- Interpretation: suggests LCHF may affect pain through mechanisms beyond weight loss, but tiny uncontrolled/crossover-like design.

## PMID 30122560 / PMCID PMC6339822
**Title:** Dietary carbohydrate intake and mortality: prospective cohort and meta-analysis. DOI: 10.1016/S2468-2667(18)30135-X.

- General-population long-term caution: U-shaped mortality association; 50–55% energy carbs lowest; <40% and >70% higher risk in cohorts.
- Source matters: replacing carbs with animal fat/protein associated with higher mortality; plant-based fat/protein substitution associated with lower mortality.
- Use as context only, not lipedema evidence; supports avoiding indefinite animal-fat-heavy keto.

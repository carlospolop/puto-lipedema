# Round 1 mechanism subresearch: genes for lymphatic/vascular/fibrosis/adipocyte phenotypes that may cause lipedema-like features

Date: 2026-07-02  
Scope: genes not necessarily studied directly in lipedema, but mechanistically tied to component phenotypes: lymphatic dysfunction/edema, vascular permeability/angiogenesis/fragility, adipocyte expansion and gynoid fat distribution, fibrosis/ECM remodeling, immune/macrophage polarization, estrogen/adipose hormonal signaling, and pain/nociception.

## Executive interpretation

* **Direct lipedema genetics remains weak/early.** The largest family sequencing study to date (31 individuals from 9 families) did **not** identify one shared causative gene across families, but supported inherited/polygenic risk and enriched categories relevant to vascular/connective tissue biology (PMID:38407896). Reviews emphasize familial clustering, female predominance, adipocyte hypertrophy, fibrosis, vascular/lymphatic changes, and pain, but no validated diagnostic gene panel for lipedema (PMID:35743063; PMID:41548036; PMID:40425048).
* **Best “mechanism candidates” are genes causing primary lymphedema/lymphatic dysplasia** (FLT4/VEGFR3, VEGFC/VEGFD pathway, FOXC2, GJC2, CCBE1, PROX1, SOX18, PIEZO1). These are not proven lipedema genes, but they are high-priority because lipedema includes edema/heaviness and reported lymphatic/vascular dysfunction; they also create clinically overlapping leg swelling phenotypes.
* **Adipocyte/adipogenesis genes are high-priority indirect candidates** (PPARG, CEBPA/B, PIK3CA/AKT1/PTEN, PLIN1, LIPE, PNPLA2, LPL, FABP4, ADIPOQ, LEP). Lipedema’s defining feature is disproportionate lower-body subcutaneous adipose expansion; lipedema tissue studies report adipocyte hypertrophy, altered lymph/angiogenesis genes, inflammation and fibrosis (PMID:37108757; PMID:39652636).
* **Hormone/steroid genes have the strongest direct gene-level lipedema signal so far**, especially **AKR1C1** and **AKR1C2** (not in the required list but important): familial WES found an AKR1C1 missense variant in autosomal-dominant nonsyndromic lipedema (PMID:32872468); AKR1C2 mutation/overexpression was proposed in another study (PMID:38112953). For the required estrogen axis, ESR1/ESR2/CYP19A1 are plausible rather than established.
* **Fibrosis/ECM and macrophage genes are medium-to-high mechanistic priorities** because lipedema tissues show progressive fibrosis/ECM remodeling and macrophage changes. MIF/CD74 has direct lipedema tissue evidence (PMID:37887430), while TGFB1/collagens/MMP/TIMP/LOX/FN1/POSTN and CD163/MRC1/CCL2/TNF/IL6/IL10/NLRP3 are strong indirect biology.
* **Pain genes are plausibility candidates, not known lipedema genes.** SCN9A/TRPV1/NGF map well to neuropathic/inflammatory pain and nociceptor sensitization, consistent with painful/tender lipedema, but direct lipedema genetic evidence is minimal.

## Notes by mechanism domain

### 1) Lymphatic dysfunction and lymphedema-overlap genes
The highest-confidence causal lymphatic genes are not proven lipedema genes, but they define the biology most likely to create lipedema-like swelling:

* **VEGFC/VEGFD–FLT4/VEGFR3 axis**: central lymphangiogenic signaling; FLT4 variants cause Milroy/primary lymphedema-like disease. If lipedema patients have reduced lymph/angiogenesis gene expression in thigh SAT (reported in PMID:39652636), this axis becomes a rational expression and rare-variant target.
* **FOXC2**: lymphatic valve development; plausible for dependent edema and venous/lymphatic incompetence.
* **PROX1/SOX18**: lymphatic endothelial identity/specification. Pathogenic variants more likely produce syndromic lymphatic disease than isolated lipedema, but mild/modifier variants could contribute to susceptibility.
* **CCBE1**: VEGFC processing/lymphangiogenesis; classically Hennekam lymphangiectasia-lymphedema syndrome.
* **GJC2**: connexin-mediated lymphatic function; primary lymphedema pedigrees.
* **PIEZO1**: mechanosensitive ion channel required for vascular/lymphatic development/function; variants cause generalized lymphatic dysplasia/hydrops-spectrum phenotypes.

Interpretation: these genes should be treated as **differential diagnosis/subtype/modifier candidates** for lipedema, lipo-lymphedema, or familial leg swelling rather than as established lipedema genes.

### 2) Vascular permeability, angiogenesis, bruising
Lipedema vascular reviews describe endothelial dysfunction, aberrant angiogenesis, vessel fragility and fluid accumulation (PMID:42181679). Candidate genes:

* **VEGFA**: angiogenesis/permeability; can support adipose expansion via vascular supply but also edema.
* **ANGPT1/ANGPT2 and TEK/TIE2**: endothelial quiescence vs activation/leak; relevant to vessel fragility and vascular malformation biology.
* Interaction with lymphatic genes matters: excess leakage plus insufficient lymphatic clearance yields chronic interstitial fluid, inflammation, and fibrosis.

### 3) Adipocyte expansion and regional fat distribution
Lipedema’s central phenotype is lower-body/gynoid SAT expansion. Required candidates:

* **PPARG, CEBPA, CEBPB**: master adipogenic transcription factors.
* **PIK3CA/AKT1/PTEN**: growth, insulin signaling, adipogenesis; also vascular/adipose overgrowth when mosaic.
* **LPL**: fatty-acid uptake into adipose; **PLIN1, LIPE, PNPLA2**: lipid droplet protection and lipolysis; **FABP4**: adipocyte lipid handling; **ADIPOQ/LEP**: adipokine output.

Direct lipedema support is mostly phenotype/tissue-level, not gene-variant-level. A 2025 Diabetes study found women with obesity + lipedema had greater leg fat mass, lower android:gynoid ratio, greater insulin sensitivity, and thigh-vs-abdominal differences: greater macrophage content/inflammation/fibrosis gene expression and lower lymph/angiogenesis-related genes (PMID:39652636).

### 4) Estrogen/hormonal signaling
Required genes:

* **ESR1/ESR2**: estrogen receptor signaling in adipocytes, vasculature, immune cells.
* **CYP19A1/aromatase**: local estrogen production in adipose.
* Add **AKR1C1/AKR1C2** because lipedema-specific studies implicate steroid metabolism.

Systematic review evidence supports hormonal hypotheses but notes limited and heterogeneous data (PMID:41575573). Therefore ESR1/ESR2/CYP19A1 are **high-priority plausibility genes** but currently weaker than AKR1C1/AKR1C2 for direct lipedema gene evidence.

### 5) Fibrosis and ECM remodeling
Lipedema and lymphedema share “fluid, fat, and fibrosis” but with different mechanisms/timing (PMID:35743063). Candidate genes:

* **TGFB1**: canonical pro-fibrotic cytokine.
* **COL1A1/COL3A1/COL6A1-A3**: structural collagens; COL6 especially relevant to adipose ECM.
* **FN1/POSTN/LOX**: matrix deposition, remodeling, collagen crosslinking.
* **MMP2/MMP9/MMP14 and TIMP1/TIMP2**: ECM degradation/remodeling balance.

These should be prioritized for tissue expression/protein studies and stage progression, but rare germline causality is uncertain.

### 6) Immune/macrophage polarization and inflammation
Direct lipedema work points to macrophage changes rather than classic obesity-like inflammation:

* **MIF/CD74**: elevated MIF-1 and CD74 mRNA in lipedema tissue (PMID:37887430).
* **CD163/MRC1**: M2-like macrophage markers; reviews report M2 predominance in lipedema adipose tissue (PMID:41278213).
* **CCL2/TNF/IL6/IL10/NLRP3**: inflammatory recruitment/cytokine/inflammasome axis; should be analyzed by stage and depot because lipedema may differ from obesity.

### 7) Pain/nociception
Pain is central clinically but underdeveloped genetically:

* **SCN9A**: Nav1.7 pain channel; variants cause extreme human pain phenotypes.
* **TRPV1**: inflammatory heat/capsaicin nociceptor channel.
* **NGF**: nociceptor growth/sensitization and neurogenic inflammation.

These are currently best considered **pain-modifier genes/pathways** that may interact with inflammation, edema pressure, fibrosis/stiffness, and small-fiber changes.

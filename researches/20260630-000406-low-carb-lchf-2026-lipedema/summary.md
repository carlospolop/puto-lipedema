# Summary: LCHF/low-carb 2026 in lipedema

## Topic

## Key conclusions
- Best practical interpretation is Mediterranean-style low-carb, not high-saturated-fat keto.
- Direct lipedema evidence is promising but limited: small, often calorie-restricted, short/intermediate-term and sometimes uncontrolled.
- The 2026 fatty-acid analysis supports reducing saturated fatty acids such as myristic/palmitic rather than increasing them.
- The 7-month LCHF study used <50 g/day carbohydrate but also Mediterranean food quality: olive oil, fish, nuts/seeds, non-starchy vegetables and reduced processed saturated-fat foods.
- A 6-8 week moderate low-carb trial is more reasonable as first step than strict keto for most patients.
- Monitor symptoms and, when sustained/high-fat/keto: LDL, HDL, TG, glucose/HbA1c; ApoB if LDL rises or risk is present; liver/kidney/electrolytes/uric acid as context requires.

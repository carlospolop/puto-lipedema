# Source discovery notes — lipólisis grasa lipedema

## Local context already in site/researches
- Low-carb/LCHF evidence in lipedema is already summarized locally: LIPODIET and derivatives report short-term reductions in weight/fat, pain, calf SAT/circumference in a tiny MRI sub-study, but also lean/muscle loss risk; Mediterranean low-carb is preferred over saturated-fat-heavy keto.
- GLP-1/incretins page: exenatide case series n=5 in insulin-resistant lipedema and tirzepatide case report are promising but weak; use only if medical metabolic indication exists.
- Vascular/endothelial/ECM notes: lipedema tissue has microvascular permeability, interstitial fluid/GAG matrix, adipocyte hypertrophy, fibrosis, macrophages; these may impair exchange/oxygenation and make leg changes slower.
- Existing site warns Endermologie/criolipolysis are not proven lipedema treatment; shockwave may improve pain/function/elasticity more than fat.

## Mechanistic evidence map so far
- Lipolysis needs: low enough insulin, catecholamine signaling, ATGL/HSL activation, blood flow to deliver hormones and remove fatty acids/glycerol, mitochondrial oxidation sink through activity, and enough time/energy deficit.
- Leg/gluteofemoral fat in women is naturally more storage-oriented and less lipolytic than abdominal/visceral fat; lipedema may add fibrosis, edema, pain/inactivity, microvascular/lymphatic dysfunction and inflammatory remodeling.
- Therefore the target is not local spot burning but changing systemic and tissue conditions: lower insulin/energy availability, raise muscle demand, improve perfusion/edema/oxygenation, preserve muscle, reduce inflammation/ultraprocessed diet, and measure fat vs water separately.

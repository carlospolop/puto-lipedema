Audit conclusion

The source record supports “not a treatment for lipedema itself”, not “universally contraindicated in every person with lipedema.”

The distinctions are:

The 2021 U.S. Standard of Care states: “Long-term use of diuretics should be avoided in people with lipedema.” That is chronic-use avoidance, not a formal prohibition of every diuretic exposure. 
PMC

The 2024 German S2k guideline is more precise: “Diuretika sollen nicht zur Behandlung des Lipödems eingesetzt werden. Der Einsatz … aus internistischer Indikation ist möglich.” In other words, do not use them to treat lipedema, but an independent internal-medicine indication remains possible. The recommendation is ↑↑ with 94.4% agreement. 
AWMF Leitlinienregister
+1

The 2023 ISL consensus discourages long-term diuretics in peripheral lymphedema because benefit is marginal and fluid/electrolyte imbalance may occur, while reserving limited use for selected comorbidities and complications. It also distinguishes early pure lipedema from lymphatic disease and notes that lymphedema may become a later comorbidity. 
International Society of Lymphology
+1

No direct controlled clinical trial of a systemic diuretic for lipedema itself was located in the targeted search. The two tissue studies cited beneath the U.S. pathophysiology rationale were small observational studies—10 patients with lipedema and 11 matched controls in Felmerer, and 30 patients with lipedema and 19 controls in Al-Ghadban—not diuretic interventions. They cannot establish efficacy, harm, or pharmacological contraindication.

Both proposed absolutes therefore fail:

“Never use a diuretic in anyone with lipedema” is disproved by the German guideline’s explicit internal-medicine exception and by primary guidance recommending diuresis for heart-failure congestion, nephrotic edema and cirrhotic ascites. The drug then treats the comorbidity, not lipedema tissue.

The website-safe wording adopted in the report is:

Los diuréticos no tratan el tejido adiposo ni la causa principal del lipedema y no deben utilizarse con ese objetivo. Esto no significa que estén prohibidos en toda persona con lipedema: pueden ser necesarios si existe otra enfermedad que produzca sobrecarga de líquido o edema —por ejemplo, insuficiencia cardiaca, determinado edema renal o ascitis— y deben indicarse y vigilarse por el profesional que trate esa enfermedad. En el linfedema o lipolinfedema su beneficio suele ser escaso y el uso prolongado se desaconseja, salvo situaciones seleccionadas. La hinchazón venosa o causada por ciertos medicamentos puede no responder a “pastillas de agua”. Los riesgos dependen del fármaco e incluyen deshidratación, deterioro renal, alteraciones de sodio, potasio, magnesio y del equilibrio ácido-base, además de interacciones. No se recomienda iniciar, suspender ni modificar un diurético por cuenta propia.
